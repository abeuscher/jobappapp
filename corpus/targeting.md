# Targeting — Job Search Criteria

## How to use this doc

Two consumers: **me**, as a filter while manually screening listings; and later the **match/tailor stage** of the app, to pick a resume variant and angle the customization.

Two axes, kept separate on purpose:
- **Archetype** — the *role type*. Determines which resume variant leads. Three of them.
- **Domain** — the *company flavor*. A priority/scoring layer, not a gate. A role can be archetype-fit regardless of domain; domain match just moves it up the queue.

A listing is worth applying to if it clears the **hard filters** and matches **at least one archetype**. Domain is the tiebreaker and the enthusiasm multiplier.

---

## Axis 1 — Role archetypes

Each archetype maps to one resume variant (see variant map at the bottom).

### A. Founding / early engineer → variant: `generalist-builder`
Probably the highest-fit lane: reconciles "generalist," "builds things solo," and "founder-operator" in one role.

- **Titles to search:** Founding Engineer, Founding Full-Stack Engineer, Early / Early-Stage Engineer, Member of Technical Staff, Software Engineer #1–5, Full-Stack Engineer (seed / Series A).
- **Where:** YC Work at a Startup, Wellfound, VC portfolio talent boards (a16z, Sequoia, First Round, etc. — mostly Getro/Consider-powered, sitting on Greenhouse/Ashby/Lever).
- **JD signal words:** "wear many hats," "0-to-1," "generalist," "high autonomy," tiny team.
- **Lead with:** NpHelper built solo, agentic-coding workflows, range across web + CMS + AI. The founder-operator story is the differentiator here, not a footnote.

### B. LLM / AI engineer → variant: `ai-specialist`
The most differentiated, most current lane — and the one your existing resume undersells worst (AI is buried at the bottom; here it goes to the top).

- **Titles to search:** AI Engineer, Applied AI Engineer, Forward-Deployed Engineer (FDE), LLM Engineer, AI Application Engineer, Member of Technical Staff (Applied), Solutions Engineer (AI).
- **Where:** AI-specific boards, YC AI companies, model-company customers, the same VC boards filtered for AI.
- **JD signal words:** RAG, agents, evals, LLM application, prompt/context engineering, "shipping LLM features in production."
- **Lead with:** MAIA (nine LLM engines, consent-based EHR access, hackathon reference impl), multi-provider work (OpenAI/Anthropic/Gemini/Mistral), RAG, agentic workflows. This variant is a real re-weighting of the resume, not a tweak.

### C. Engineering leadership → variant: `leadership`
The founder-operator pitch. Real, but your resume's only explicit management signal is the Contentstack team-lead line, so this variant leans hardest on the corpus.

- **Titles to search:** Engineering Manager, Head of Engineering, Director of Engineering, VP Eng (small co), Founding Engineering Manager, Tech Lead.
- **Where:** 20–100-person companies on the same startup boards.
- **Lead with:** ran companies end-to-end, CEO-adjacent cross-functional scope, led the remote Contentstack team.
- **Tactical note:** at this level cold applications convert worst — leadership hires lean heavily on warm intros. Worth flagging any of these that are reachable through a human rather than a portal, and prioritizing those.

## Axis 2 — Domain preferences (priority layer)

Not filters — multipliers. A role that's archetype-fit *and* in one of these jumps the queue.

- **Entertainment** — studios, streaming, media-tech, live events, music-tech. Thrive in high-personality, creative-problem-solving environments. Increasingly remote-friendly for eng roles.
- **Gaming** — studios (web / platform / tools / live-ops eng), gaming platforms, gaming-adjacent tooling. The 2K Games line is a real door-opener; this is passion, not just past work.
- **Creative** — design tools, creative SaaS, media/publishing tech, product-led agencies. Creative context can tip the scale even when the work itself isn't thrilling.
- **Mission-aligned** — orgs whose stated mission is economic justice / financial equality / inclusion: look for B-Corp certification, public-benefit / nonprofit status, fintech-for-inclusion, labor/worker-advocacy tech, civic tech. (Match on the org's stated mission, not vibes.)

## Hard filters (auto-pass if violated)

- **Location:** remote is the default and the strong preference. Also open to: a role commutable from Amherst, MA (realistically the Springfield–Hartford corridor; Worcester/Boston-edge only as hybrid stretches — adjust radius as you see fit), and travel-based / nonstandard setups. **Relocation: off the table** *(assumption — correct if wrong)*.
- **No-go:** weapons / defense contractors; hard-right PACs or political entities. Everything else is case-by-case.

## Adjacent track — Voice-over

Working VO artist with a portfolio and paid work. Handling:
- Keep a `corpus/voiceover.md` one-pager + demo reel link as a standalone asset.
- In the three eng variants, one line under "other skills / interests" — reads as range, and is a genuine plus for entertainment / gaming / creative targets.
- If a direct VO gig ever warrants it, spin up a separate VO-specific pitch. Not now.
